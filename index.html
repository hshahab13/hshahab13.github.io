I am a first-year Ph.D. student in the School of City and Regional Planning (SCaRP) at Georgia Institute of Technology. I work as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Center for Urban Resilience and Analytics (CURA) with the Resilient and Equitable Mobility Analytics and Planning (REMAP) research group, advised by Dr. Rounaq Basu.

I earned my MS in Public Policy and Management with a Data Analytics specialization (MSPPM-DA) from Carnegie Mellon University (2022) and a BSc in Economics with a minor in Public Management from Lahore University of Management Sciences (2018). Prior to beginning my doctoral studies, I worked as a Senior Transportation Economics and Data Science Analyst in Boston, MA, where I applied economic and data-driven analyses to support transportation planning and mobility projects for state departments of transportation, federal agencies, and other public-sector organizations.

Research

My research spans across four interconnected areas:
1. Travel Behavior Analysis
2. Urban Computing and Networks
3. Advanced Methods in Modeling (Causal Inference & Econometrics)
4. Land Use & New Mobility Adoption

My work focuses on analyzing individual travel patterns and applying computational methods to address critical transportation equity challenges, including climate injustice and safety. Some highlighted research is shared below.
